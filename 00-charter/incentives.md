# Contributor Incentives

> Each person earns what they should — no less, no more.

The incentive system has three tiers. They are designed so that doing the right thing for Knowledge Gate is also the rational thing for any individual contributor.

---

## Tier 1 — Survival baseline

Anyone doing **core work** receives a fixed stipend from Knowledge Gate's commercial revenue. Core work means:

- Maintaining the protocol or governance documents.
- Serving on the Equity Layer committee.
- Producing or curating baseline (free-tier) content.

Stipend rules:
- Cap on the maximum stipend is set by the protocol; no one can quietly raise their own pay.
- Floor is set so that core contributors are not pushed to take outside work that competes for their attention.
- Stipends are public — published in this repo, updated quarterly.

## Tier 2 — Contribution points

All contributions — uploaded content, plugin code, governance participation, protocol maintenance — are recorded permanently as **contribution points**. Points convert to:

- A share of platform profits.
- Honors and certifications.
- Eligibility for committee seats.

Rules:
- Points are awarded by transparent rubric; rubric itself is in this repo and amendable by ADR.
- Points cannot be revoked retroactively except for fraud, and only by Protocol Layer ruling.
- Points outlive membership: leaving Knowledge Gate does not erase your record.

## Tier 3 — Exit protection

Any contributor leaving Knowledge Gate retains:

- Their permanent point record.
- Authorship attribution that cannot be removed.
- Income from sources outside Knowledge Gate (the protocol does not non-compete against you).

No one may be punished for leaving. No one loses past credit.

---

## Founder / early contributors

All founding contributors — including the charter's original author — hold **no special privilege** in this system. They have only responsibilities and the same earned return.

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Up-front investment | Recorded transparently in the initial agreement, public, applies to everyone equally |
| Repayment | After the platform becomes profitable, repaid per protocol with priority, then equal terms with all contributors thereafter |
| Voting weight | Same as anyone else once committees form |
| Salary multiplier | None |
| Termination penalty | None — same exit terms as anyone else |

---

## Disputes

A contributor who believes their points were mis-awarded:

1. Opens an issue in this repo with rubric reference.
2. Equity Layer reviews within 14 days.
3. Either side may escalate to the Protocol Layer; ruling is final and published as an ADR.
